Power-ups & Upgrades — Complete Reference

Loop Runner has two parallel progression systems: permanent roguelite upgrades that auto-pick when you cross score thresholds, and temporary power-ups dropped by certain enemies that buff you for a short window. They stack. They synergise. Drawing the right pair early can carry an otherwise average run into podium territory.

Permanent upgrades are auto-applied silently when you cross thresholds — they used to require a click, which meant pausing to read cards mid-fight and was the most common cause of "stupid deaths." The auto-pick redesign landed in the 8 May 2026 update. Temporary power-ups fall as pickups and time out after a short window.

Permanent upgrades

These auto-roll at score milestones from a pool of upgrades you haven't already maxed. Roll order is deterministic per-seed (so the Daily Run hands every player the same upgrades in the same sequence), but pseudo-random in Normal mode. Most upgrades stack: drawing the same one twice improves it incrementally.

Ring Burst Permanent

Every Nth fired round emits a circular shock-ring that damages every enemy within radius. The visual tell is a brief expanding ring; the audio is a low woomp. At higher stacks the ring fires more frequently and reaches further.

Synergy: Pairs naturally with anything that boosts kill count per second — Quick Reload, Heavy Caliber. The ring's damage benefits from the combo multiplier on every enemy it clears, so a Ring Burst proc during a hot chain can score four-figure points from a single shot.

Piercing Rounds Permanent

Bullets pass through their first hit and continue to the next. Damage drops slightly per pierce so a single round won't usually one-shot a column of three, but it'll soften them all enough that the next pair of shots finishes the cluster.

Synergy: Best with swarm-heavy themes — Cyberpunk's alien cells, Deep Sea's piranhas, Mythical's goblins, Cosmic's meteors. A Piercing Round through five swarm enemies in a row is the single most efficient chain extension in the game.

Quick Reload Permanent

Cuts magazine recharge time. Stacks: each level reduces the timer further. At high stacks rounds return almost as fast as you can fire them, effectively removing magazine economy from your decision tree.

Synergy: Universal — there's no run where Quick Reload is wasted. Especially valuable early (before you've drawn other upgrades) and late (when wave density punishes any delay). Many top players consider this the strongest single upgrade in the pool.

Heavy Caliber Permanent

Each round deals more damage. At base levels it turns two-shot enemies into one-shot enemies; at high stacks it eats meaningfully into boss HP per round.

Synergy: Pairs with Piercing Rounds to one-shot multiple enemies in a column. Pairs with Critical Hits for occasional massive single-target damage spikes. The Heavy Caliber + Critical Hits combination is the "boss melter" build.

Time Warp Permanent

Slows enemy movement and projectile velocity by a percentage. Your own movement and fire rate stay normal. The effect is subtle at base levels and dramatic at stack 3+.

Synergy: Lifeline upgrade. The pool's only "make the game easier" effect — useful when the rest of your build is offensive and the screen is getting too busy to track. Strong on mobile, where input latency makes fast projectiles hardest to dodge.

Powerup Magnet Permanent

Increases the pickup radius for dropped power-ups. At base level you have to be near the drop; at high stacks they fly toward you from across the screen.

Synergy: Builds on itself — collecting more temporary power-ups means more Shield/Rapid/Magnet uptime, which means more kills, which means more drops. The single best "snowball" upgrade in the pool. Especially valuable if you've already drawn Greed.

Greed Permanent

Multiplies the base score of every kill. Doesn't change damage, doesn't change spawn rate, just inflates the points-per-enemy. Stacks multiplicatively with combo, so the chain multiplier on a Greed-boosted kill is enormous.

Synergy: Pure score upgrade — strongest on long runs where you'd already be alive at high combo levels. Less valuable on a run that's going to die at 5,000 anyway. Pair with Critical Hits and Heavy Caliber for the dedicated score-maximising build.

Critical Hits Permanent

A percentage chance per shot to land a critical — typically 2× damage and 2× score on that kill. Each stack raises the crit chance.

Synergy: Loves chain depth. A crit on a combo-40 kill is worth more than every shot of the first minute combined. Stack alongside Greed for a score-focused build, or alongside Heavy Caliber for an HP-shredding boss-killer build.

Health Up Permanent

Restores a fraction of HP on roll. Less of an upgrade and more of a heal — it doesn't raise your maximum, just patches you back up if you've taken a hit.

Synergy: A "thank you" roll when you're already at full HP — the score milestones that grant upgrades are usually quiet moments, so getting Health Up at full health is mostly wasted. Still better than no roll. If you've taken a hit it's effectively a second life.

Bullet Speed Permanent

Increases projectile travel speed. Makes leading less necessary on moving targets, especially chargers and orbiters.

Synergy: Universal quality-of-life. Doesn't carry a run by itself but makes every other offensive upgrade more reliable. Best when your aim is the limiting factor — strong on mobile, less critical on desktop where mouse precision is already high.

Temporary power-ups (enemy drops)

These appear as visual pickups when certain enemies die. They time out after a short window — see the visible ring or pulse around your ship for the remaining duration. Stacking the same pickup twice usually extends the timer rather than doubling the effect.

Shield Temporary

Absorbs one hit. The shield pulse is visible around your ship — a faint ring that brightens when active and flashes when broken. The single most chain-saving pickup in the game; a shield is the difference between a death and a continued 60-combo chain.

Synergy: Always pick up. There's no reason not to. The only situation where you skip a Shield is if grabbing it costs your combo timer — and that's a knife-edge call.

Rapid Fire Temporary

Temporarily uncaps the magazine — fire as fast as you click for the duration. The single best chain-builder pickup. A Rapid Fire window dropped during a hot combo turns a 30-chain into a 60-chain.

Synergy: Bonkers with Piercing Rounds (Rapid spam through swarms), bonkers with Heavy Caliber (faster boss DPS). The single window where Rapid is wasted is during a quiet spawn lull — you can't fire what isn't there.

Magnet Temporary

Pulls all on-screen pickups toward you for the duration. Effectively a wide-radius vacuum. Different from the permanent Powerup Magnet upgrade — this is an active pickup, not a stat.

Synergy: Grab and forget. The temporary Magnet stacking with the permanent Powerup Magnet creates a brief "everything on screen is yours" moment that's genuinely satisfying — and pulls in any Shield or Rapid drops you'd otherwise have missed.

How rolls are decided

When you cross a score threshold, the game silently rolls a random upgrade from the pool of upgrades you haven't already maxed. The roll skips Health Up if you're at full HP on a hot chain. The chosen upgrade applies with a banner and screen-shake flourish — the visual feedback is loud, but the gameplay continuity is preserved: cursor stays where you left it, no modal, no click required.

The old design was a card-pick modal where you had to mouse over and click a card. Players consistently moved the cursor into the spawn area and died on resume — see the 8 May 2026 devlog for the rationale behind the redesign. If you miss the old version, you're rare; almost everyone preferred the auto-pick.

Drafting a build — which combinations to hope for

The chain build

Quick Reload + Piercing Rounds + Greed + Critical Hits. The score-maximising build. Quick Reload keeps rounds available, Piercing chains through swarms, Greed inflates per-kill, Critical Hits spikes individual numbers. Reliable 25,000+ runs when all four land in the first ten thousand points of score.

The boss-melter build

Heavy Caliber + Critical Hits + Bullet Speed. Optimised for one-shotting bosses and shielders. Weaker on swarm pressure but turns every milestone wave into a 2-second fight. Survives long enough to circle all themes if you can manage swarm pressure with movement instead of damage.

The survival build

Time Warp + Powerup Magnet + Shield uptime. Defensive. Slows the screen, vacuums every Shield drop into range, prioritises staying alive over score per kill. Best for new players learning the late-game pressure curve.

The lucky-rolls build

Whatever you draw. Most runs aren't going to land four ideal rolls. The honest truth is that almost any three upgrades, applied to a player who's already chain-disciplined, will get to 20,000. The build matters less than the habits — see the Strategy Guide for the habits.

What's not in the pool

Some things are deliberately not upgrades. There's no upgrade that buys lives, no upgrade that scales the player ship, no upgrade that adds a second weapon slot. The design constraint is that every run must be readable from the score and combo pills alone — adding HUD complexity for extra build state would dilute the focus on the chain.

We also don't sell upgrades. There's no in-game shop, no premium currency, no boosts you can pay for to start a run with extras. The leaderboard is honest because everyone has the same upgrade pool and the same random rolls. See the About page for the design philosophy.
