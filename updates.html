Release notes, balance tweaks, and behind-the-scenes design notes. Latest first.

2026-05-08 — Eight rotating themes, vector enemy redesign, and boss waves

The biggest visual overhaul since launch. Every enemy in the game has been redrawn from scratch as a custom vector creature, and a new theme cycle rotates the whole cast every three minutes through eight distinct eras.

The eight themes (in cycle order)

  • Jurassic — T-Rex, Triceratops, Velociraptor, Ankylosaurus, Pterosaur, Dilophosaurus.
  • Cyberpunk — neon-vector sci-fi: 8-pointed star, alien cell, predator dart, hex hull, drone, eye-turret.
  • Deep Sea — jellyfish, pufferfish, shark, hermit crab, piranha, anglerfish (with a glowing lure).
  • Underworld — skull, bubbling cauldron, horned demon, tombstone, bat, sad ghost.
  • Mythical — goblin with a club, hydra, lance-charging knight, plate-armor knight, fairy with a star wand, wizard's eye orb.
  • Cosmic — asteroid, black hole, comet, ringed planet, meteor, satellite with a tracking dish.
  • Glitch — RGB-split shapes, datamoshed cursor, encrypted hex cube, surveillance camera.
  • Steampunk — cogwheel, ticking clockwork bomb, steam locomotive, riveted iron cube, brass dirigible, mechanical iris-aperture eye.

Survive the full 24-minute revolution and a special "You've circled all themes" banner fires.

Boss waves

At score milestones 5,000, 15,000, and 30,000 a single tougher enemy spawns: 2.4× the size of a regular foe, 8–18 HP (visible HP bar above the body), and 6× score reward. The boss inherits a random AI archetype from charger / shielder / orbiter, so what you fight changes per run — and theme-renders for free, so a 30k boss in Glitch is a giant RGB-split datamoshed cursor while in Steampunk it's a 2.4× brass mechanical iris-aperture eye.

Player ship

Replaced the white player dot with a custom-drawn vector dart-ship. Smooth-rotates to face the cursor, banks slightly into hard turns, fires a continuous mint-green particle thrust trail, and an animated flame plume off the engine. Bullets are now comet streaks oriented along velocity instead of round dots.

Death VFX upgrade

Kills now eject 6–9 spinning triangular debris shards in the enemy's core color, after a brief pre-burst white flash pulse — the same flash-then-debris microbeat AAA games use to make explosions feel weighty.

2026-05-08 — Hall of Fame, country leaderboards, and UX polish

A run of smaller features that all hit on the same day.

Hall of Fame

The new Hall of Fame page archives the top 3 finishers from every Daily Run, going back through history. Gold/silver/bronze medals, today highlighted, summary chip showing total days archived. Pure client-side — fetches the latest 500 daily submissions, buckets them by date, picks the podium per day.

Country leaderboards

The leaderboard modal grew a Scope select. Switch between Worldwide and Your Country — country is detected from the same IP geolocation already used to flag scores. Daily and country scopes combine cleanly. If geolocation is blocked or unresolved, country scope falls back to worldwide with a hint.

Upgrade picks now auto-apply

The roguelite upgrade modal that popped at score thresholds (Ring Burst, Pierce, Quick Reload, etc.) was forcing players to move the cursor to click a card — which then teleported the player into wherever you clicked, often into an enemy. Upgrades now silently roll a random one from the pool you haven't maxed, applied with a banner + flash + shake. No interruption, cursor stays where you left it.

Mobile pause button

Added a 48×48px ⏸ button bottom-left during play. Mobile players had no Esc/P key — pausing was desktop-only.

Other polish

  • Personal history chart in the leaderboard modal — last 20 runs as scaled bars, mint for Normal mode and gold for Daily.
  • Live ticker at the top of the leaderboard — "🔥 anon just scored 4,230 in PT" notifications fade in/out as new scores land.
  • Empty-fire feedback — firing on an empty mag now plays a dud SFX and pulses the recharging slot red.
  • Network hardening — leaderboard, rank, and geo fetches now timeout in 3–5s and fall through to local cache instead of hanging if a network blocker silently drops a request.
  • Post-death canvas freeze — 1.5s after dying, the render loop returns without redrawing. GPU goes idle under the leaderboard modal instead of repainting frozen state at 60fps.
  • Performance fixes — cosmic and underworld themes were causing slowdowns / browser freezes from per-frame gradient allocations and stacked shadow-blur passes on high-spawn enemies. Both rewritten to solid additive triangles and single shadow passes.
