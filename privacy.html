Privacy Policy

This policy explains what information Playloop (the operator of playloop.run) collects when you play Loop Runner, how that information is used and shared, and what rights you have over it. We've tried to write it in plain English. If anything is unclear, please ask: privacy@playloop.run.

Last updated: 17 May 2026 · Effective: 17 May 2026

1. The short version

  • We don't require accounts. The only personal identifier we ever ask for is a display name on the leaderboard, which is whatever you choose to type — your real name or a pseudonym.
  • We use Google AdSense to display advertisements. Google may use cookies and device identifiers to serve and measure ads. EU, UK, EEA, and Swiss visitors see a consent prompt (Google Funding Choices) on the first visit, where ad personalisation can be declined in one click.
  • We use Plausible Analytics to count page views. Plausible does not use cookies, does not store IP addresses, and does not fingerprint visitors.
  • Leaderboard scores are stored in a Supabase (Postgres) database. We store the score, the display name you entered, an approximate country derived from your IP, and a submission timestamp. We do not store IP addresses, precise location, email addresses, or any payment data — we don't take payments at all.
  • You can request access to, correction of, or deletion of any data we hold about you at any time. See section 9.

2. Who we are

"Playloop," "we," "us," and "our" mean the operator of the website at https://playloop.run/. Playloop is an independent publisher. Contact details are in section 12.

4. What we don't collect

  • We don't ask for or store your email address, real name, address, phone number, or date of birth.
  • We don't take payments and we don't store payment information.
  • We don't collect precise geolocation. The country flag is derived from an IP-to-country lookup with no city-or-finer detail.
  • We don't run device fingerprinting. We don't run session-replay tools. We don't use heatmap software.
  • We don't sell data. We don't share data with brokers or list-rental services. We don't run "data partnerships."
  • We don't push notifications. The site will never ask for permission to send you push messages.

5. Third parties we use

Google AdSense (advertising)

We use Google AdSense to serve advertisements in three placements: a top banner, an interstitial between runs, and a unit inside the leaderboard modal. Ads are clearly labelled as ads and never overlay live gameplay.

Google, as a third-party vendor, may use cookies, web beacons, device identifiers, and similar technologies to serve ads based on a user's prior visits to this and other websites. Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on user visits to Playloop and other sites on the internet. Users may opt out of personalised advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings or aboutads.info.

For visitors in the EEA, UK, and Switzerland we use Google's Funding Choices Consent Management Platform (CMP), which presents an IAB TCF-compliant consent prompt on first visit. You can decline personalised advertising entirely; you will still see non-personalised ads. You can revisit your consent choice at any time using your browser's site settings (clear site data for playloop.run) to re-trigger the prompt.

Google's own privacy policy describes how Google handles data on its end: policies.google.com/privacy. Google's advertising-specific terms are at policies.google.com/technologies/ads.

Plausible Analytics (analytics)

We use Plausible Analytics, a privacy-friendly, EU-hosted analytics provider. Plausible does not use cookies, does not collect or store IP addresses, does not fingerprint visitors, and does not generate persistent identifiers. The data we see is limited to aggregate counts: which pages are viewed, where visitors come from at a country level, and which browser families are used. Plausible's own privacy notice is at plausible.io/privacy.

Supabase (leaderboard database)

The Loop Runner leaderboard is stored in a Supabase Postgres database. Supabase acts as a data processor on our behalf. The only data ever written to it is the score, your chosen display name, the game mode, the submission timestamp, and the two-letter country code. Supabase's privacy policy is at supabase.com/privacy.

GitHub Pages (web hosting)

The static site files are hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub processes routine server logs (including IP addresses) for security and abuse prevention. GitHub's privacy statement is at docs.github.com/en/site-policy/privacy-policies/github-general-privacy-statement.

IP-to-country lookup (country flags)

To show the right country flag next to a leaderboard entry, we make a single request from your browser to a public IP-to-country lookup service. The service receives your IP in the course of the request but we don't store it. Only the resolved two-letter country code (e.g. "GB") is sent to our database.

6. Cookies and similar technologies

We don't set first-party tracking cookies. Our own JavaScript uses your browser's localStorage to remember your display name, personal best score, and run history chart between sessions — this data never leaves your device.

Google AdSense may set cookies as part of advertising. The specific cookies depend on whether you consented to personalisation and which ad creatives you saw, but typical cookies include:

  • NID, IDE, ANID, DSID — Google advertising identifiers used for ad selection, measurement, and frequency capping
  • __gpi, __gads — Google publisher identifiers used to limit how often you see a given ad
  • FCCDCF — Funding Choices consent record (so you don't see the prompt every page load)

You can manage cookies in your browser settings — most modern browsers let you block third-party cookies entirely, or block cookies per site. Doing so will not break the game.

7. International data transfers

Playloop is operated from the United Kingdom. The third parties we use (Google, Plausible, Supabase, GitHub) operate globally and may process data in jurisdictions outside the EEA and UK, including the United States. Each of these providers maintains its own legal mechanisms for cross-border transfers — typically Standard Contractual Clauses for EEA-to-third-country flows, plus their participation in any applicable frameworks (e.g. EU-US Data Privacy Framework). Their respective privacy notices linked in section 5 describe the specifics.

8. Children

Loop Runner is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13. If a parent or guardian believes that a child has provided us with information (for example, a leaderboard entry under their real name), please contact privacy@playloop.run and we will remove it.

For visitors in the EEA, the minimum age for consenting to data processing under the GDPR is 16 in most member states. Below that age, parental consent is required. We rely on Google's consent management platform for ad personalisation and on the parent/guardian to supervise use of the site.

9. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you have some or all of the following rights:

  • Right of access — you can ask us what data we hold about you. For most users this will be: the display name you entered and the scores attached to it.
  • Right of rectification — you can ask us to correct inaccurate data.
  • Right of erasure — you can ask us to delete your data. We will delete the matching leaderboard entries within 30 days. Local data on your own device (localStorage) is yours to clear from your browser settings.
  • Right to object — you can object to processing based on legitimate interest.
  • Right to withdraw consent — for ad personalisation, use the Funding Choices prompt or your Google Ads settings.
  • Right to data portability — your data with us is so minimal that "portability" is essentially a copy of your name and a list of your scores; we can email this on request.
  • California residents (CCPA / CPRA): you have a right to know what personal information we collect, to delete it, to correct it, and to opt out of "sale" or "sharing." We do not sell or share personal information in the CCPA/CPRA sense of those terms.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@playloop.run. We will respond within 30 days. We may ask a follow-up question to identify which leaderboard entry is yours — we have no other way to verify identity since we don't run accounts.

If you believe we have not handled your data responsibly, you have a right to lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority. In the UK that's the Information Commissioner's Office (ico.org.uk).

10. Leaderboard names — what to consider

A leaderboard entry shows the display name you typed, your score, the game mode, and your country flag. The display name is shown publicly. Two practical implications:

  • Don't put personal information in your display name. Specifically: don't use a full real name, an email address, a phone number, or anything else you wouldn't write on a billboard. We can't undo a leaderboard submission silently — deletion is possible, but the entry was public for at least a moment.
  • Don't use a name designed to harass others. We reserve the right to delete entries with offensive, harassing, hateful, or sexually explicit display names. See the Terms of Use.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy when our practices change or when a legal requirement makes an update necessary. The "Last updated" date at the top of the page reflects the most recent revision. For material changes, we will surface a notice on the site for at least 14 days after the change. Continuing to use Loop Runner after a policy update means you accept the revised policy.
