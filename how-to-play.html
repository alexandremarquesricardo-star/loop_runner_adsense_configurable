How to Play Loop Runner

Everything you need to know to actually play — controls on every device, what every HUD element means, how scoring works at a glance, what the modes are, and what to do when something doesn't behave the way you expect. If you've already played a few runs and want the deep strategy, skip ahead to the Strategy Guide.

The 30-second version

Move with your cursor. Right-click to fire — you have three rounds that recharge automatically. Don't get hit. Kill enemies in quick succession to build a combo multiplier and score grows fast. At score 5,000, 15,000, and 30,000 a boss wave spawns. Beat them, survive the eight rotating visual themes, and post your score to the leaderboard.

That's the entire game. Everything below is detail.

Desktop controls

  • Move: Mouse cursor. The ship lerps toward the cursor rather than snapping — there's a small lag baked in so movement reads as physical, not telekinetic. Strong players learn to park the cursor slightly ahead of where they want the ship.
  • Fire: Right-click (mouse button 2). You have three rounds, displayed as recharging slots in the HUD. Each fired round triggers an individual recharge timer; you can fire all three in quick succession, but you'll then have to wait for them to come back one at a time.
  • Pause: P key or Esc. Pause is a hard freeze of the game world — physics, particles, and AI all stop. Resume reopens via the same key or the Resume button on the overlay.
  • Restart: Spacebar (after death). On death the overlay automatically appears; Space is the keyboard shortcut for Play Again.
  • Open leaderboard: Leaderboard button in the HUD, top-right area.

Mobile and touch controls

  • Move: Drag with one finger anywhere on the canvas. The ship follows your finger position, not the relative drag delta — that means a long-distance touch teleports the ship's target instantly, but the ship still lerps toward it.
  • Fire: Tap with a second finger, ideally on the opposite half of the screen from your movement finger. Same three-round magazine economy as desktop.
  • Pause: 48×48px ⏸ button bottom-left, visible during play. Mobile users had no Esc/P shortcut and were dying to "wanted to pause but couldn't" — see the May 2026 update for the addition.
  • Restart: Play Again button on the death overlay (no spacebar on mobile, obviously).

The on-screen pause button is deliberately large because mobile players grip their phones in different positions and a small target is unreliable. If you find the layout cramped, switch your phone to landscape orientation — the canvas reflows and the pause button moves to follow.

The HUD — what every pill means

  • Score: Total points this run. Updates live as you kill and survive.
  • Combo: Current chain length. Flickers/dims as the decay timer winds down. When it hits zero the chain resets and the pill briefly dims. See the Strategy Guide for combo theory.
  • Best: Your personal best in this game mode on this browser. Reads from localStorage, so it's per-device unless you submit to the leaderboard.
  • Daily: Your best score on today's Daily Run seed. Resets at 00:00 UTC.
  • Privacy: Quick link to the privacy policy — required to be one click away with ads on the page.
  • Leaderboard: Opens the modal with worldwide and country rankings.

Scoring at a glance

There are three sources of points, with wildly different magnitudes:

  1. Combo kills — by far the dominant source. Each kill grants a small base value multiplied by your current combo level. The multiplier compounds: a 60-chain produces 1,830× a single fresh kill, not 60× it.
  2. Boss bonuses — a large flat reward for defeating a boss enemy at a milestone (5,000 / 15,000 / 30,000). Worth roughly 6× a normal kill at the current multiplier.
  3. Passive trickle — a few points per second of survival. Deliberately tiny so that camping doesn't score.

The takeaway: every habit that protects the combo (don't waste rounds, don't break the chain for a power-up, don't chase a far target you can't reach) is worth more than every habit that chases raw kill counts.

Permanent upgrades like Greed and Critical Hits inflate per-kill values further. See the Power-ups page for the full reference.

Modes

Normal

Endless survival with randomised spawn order. Spawn density ramps with elapsed time and score. No reset until you die. Personal best is tracked in localStorage as 'Best.'

Daily Run

A fixed seed regenerates each day at 00:00 UTC. Every player on the seed faces identical enemies in the same order at the same speed — so the Daily leaderboard rewards skill, not luck. Multiple attempts are allowed; only your best score for the day is submitted.

Yesterday's top three finishers are permanently archived in the Hall of Fame. Today's leaderboard is visible inside the leaderboard modal under the "Daily" mode select.

Power-ups and upgrades

Two parallel systems. Permanent upgrades auto-roll when you cross score thresholds — examples include Quick Reload, Ring Burst, Piercing Rounds, Heavy Caliber, Greed, Critical Hits, Time Warp, Powerup Magnet, Bullet Speed, Health Up. Temporary power-ups drop as pickups from certain enemies and time out after a short window — Shield, Rapid Fire, Magnet.

The full reference with synergy notes lives on the Power-ups page. The short version: Quick Reload is the most universally useful permanent upgrade, Shield is the most chain-saving temporary pickup, and you should grab every pickup you can reach without losing a combo timer.

The eight themes

The whole enemy cast redraws every three minutes. Eight themes rotate in this order: Jurassic → Cyberpunk → Deep Sea → Underworld → Mythical → Cosmic → Glitch → Steampunk. Each theme has its own six enemies, drawn in its own palette and silhouettes. Mechanics under the hood are consistent — a chaser is a chaser regardless of whether it's a T-Rex or a Steam Locomotive — but the visual recalibration at every transition is part of the difficulty.

Survive the full 24-minute revolution and a You've Circled All Themes banner fires. See the Themes page for the per-enemy field guide.

Accessibility notes

  • Colour-blind safety: Important game state — combo, score, magazine — is conveyed by shape and animation as well as colour. The combo HUD pill dims and flickers when the decay timer is running out regardless of palette. Theme palettes are visual flavour only; no enemy is differentiated by colour alone within a single theme.
  • Photosensitivity: Glitch theme uses chromatic-aberration effects and occasional pixel artefacts. If that's uncomfortable, the rotation will pass through Glitch in three minutes — pause briefly during minutes 18:00–21:00 if needed.
  • Keyboard-only play: Movement is mouse-only; there is no keyboard movement input. Players who can't use a pointing device cannot play the game in its current form — we're aware that's a real limitation. Reach out at hello@playloop.run if you have suggestions.
  • Reduced motion: The game does not currently respect prefers-reduced-motion. The particle systems are core to readability (bullet tracers, enemy death debris, thrust trail), so a reduced-motion mode would change gameplay rather than just style. On the roadmap.
  • Screen readers: All UI buttons and the leaderboard table use semantic HTML with aria labels. The game canvas itself, of course, isn't accessible to screen readers — that's a fundamental limitation of action games.

Performance and browser support

  • Renderer: Plain Canvas2D, no WebGL. Works in every browser made in the last decade.
  • Recommended: Any modern Chromium-based browser or Firefox. Safari works fine. Edge is identical to Chrome under the hood.
  • If you see frame drops: Close background tabs (especially video-playing ones), make sure hardware acceleration is enabled in your browser, and try fullscreen. The May 2026 perf pass rewrote the Cosmic and Underworld themes to drop per-frame gradient allocations and stacked shadow-blur passes — if you remember either theme stuttering on your machine, that's fixed.
  • Post-death freeze: 1.5 seconds after dying, the render loop pauses redrawing. This is intentional — it idles the GPU under the leaderboard modal instead of repainting a frozen scene at 60fps. You'll notice the canvas goes static; that's the optimisation, not a bug.
  • Network: Leaderboard, rank, and geolocation fetches each have 3–5 second timeouts and fall back to local cache. If a network blocker silently drops a request, the game won't hang waiting — it'll continue with cached data and surface a quiet hint. The full game logic runs locally; only leaderboard submission needs the network.

Common issues

"My score didn't appear on the leaderboard"

Three causes. Either you didn't set a display name before the run (check the input on the start overlay), the submission timed out on a flaky connection (the score is saved locally and will retry next time the leaderboard fetches), or the score is below the visible cutoff. Open the leaderboard modal — the "Your best" line at the top will show what's actually registered for you, even if it's not in the visible top-N list.

"I can't right-click to fire"

The context menu is suppressed on the game canvas only, not on the rest of the page. If your browser still shows it, an extension is overriding our handler — try fullscreen, which gives the canvas first claim on input. Some Linux + Firefox combinations have a known middle-click-paste interaction; left-click + Shift is an undocumented backup binding on those setups.

"The game is too fast / too slow"

The game runs on a fixed timestep regardless of monitor refresh rate, so speed should be consistent across hardware. If you're on a high-Hz display (144Hz+) and things feel jittery, that's a rendering artefact, not a simulation one — the game state is correct underneath. A browser hardware acceleration toggle often resolves it.

"I'm on a slow connection"

The page is ~200KB compressed before the canvas spins up. There are no large assets and no video. The leaderboard fetch is a single small JSON request. If your connection times out, you'll still be able to play offline — only leaderboard submission needs the network.

What to do next

  • You've never played: hit Play and ignore everything below 5,000. Get familiar with the controls.
  • You've played a few runs: read the Strategy Guide — combo theory is the single biggest lever.
  • You're consistently breaking 10,000: read the Themes page to learn which enemy archetype is killing your runs.
  • You want to chase a podium: the Power-ups page covers build drafting; the Daily Run is the most realistic path to a public top-three finish.
